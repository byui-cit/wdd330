Self Evaluation

Description

You will be asked to do a self evaluation of your progress on the outcomes of the course two times throughout the semester. One at the halfway point, and again at the end. You will report your progress on a 1-4 scale. The meaning of each score is below:

Advanced Understanding (4)
represents outstanding understanding, application, and integration of subject material and extensive evidence of original thinking, skillful use of concepts, and ability to analyze and solve complex problems.
Proficient (3)
represents considerable/significant understanding, application, and incorporation of the material which would prepare a student to be successful in next level courses, graduate school or employment.
Progressing (2)
represents sufficient understanding of subject matter. The student demonstrates minimal initiative to be prepared for class. Sequenced courses could be attempted, but mastering new materials might prove challenging.
Beginning (1)
represents poor performance and initiative to learn and understand and apply course materials. Retaking a course or remediation may be necessary to prepare for additional instruction in this subject matter.

Before you begin your evaluation, you should complete the following exercise:

iNaturalist

  1. Connect to the iNaturalist API. Information found here: https://api.inaturalist.org/v1/docs/
  2. Create a form that allow users to search for places in the API.
  3. Request the identifications based on their selection of a place.
  4. Display the name and image of each identification returned in a nice format.
  5. STRETCH: Many of the identifications will be duplicates. De-duplicate your results before displaying them.
  6. STRETCH: Add the ability to also filter by observation date.

As you complete this evaluation keep the following in mind:

  1. The midterm evaluation has very little impact on your final grade. Please be honest in your evaluation...do not respond with the scores that you think will get you the grade you would like, rather put down the score that best decribes where you are currently at. If you do then the midterm evaluation can work as a roadmap for the rest of the semester to tell you where you might need to spend extra time to bring your mastery up as necessary.
  2. Keep in mind your experience with the iNaturalist exercise. How did you do? Success with that will involve many of the outcomes for the course. Were you able to navigate the documentation well enough to successfully pull data from the API? Were you able to interprete your results to find the particular information you needed? Did you successfully troubleshoot and debug any errors you found? How well you succeeded with this exercise should tell you a lot about where you are in your masteries.
  3. Turn in a your code for the exercise along with your evaluation scores. This can be a github link or a zip file.
